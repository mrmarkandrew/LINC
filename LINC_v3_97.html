About LINC

Live Integrated Navigation & Coordination

How it works

LINC is a shared live map for coordinating units during an operation. Add a unit from a callsign preset or a custom name, then drag its pin to keep its position current.

Tips

  • All map tools — zoom, fit/locate, draw, day/night, terrain, Mattson Consensus — now live together in one panel, top-right of the map.
  • The small panel bottom-left shows local time, sunrise/sunset, and current conditions at the active incident's location — whichever incident (Missing Person, Critical, or Planned Operation) was most recently updated.
  • Double-click, or right-click, anywhere on the map to add a unit or a point of interest.
  • Points of interest can use a preset type — TMP, Command Post, Victim, RZ, Offender, Offender's Vehicle, VID, Exhibit — each with its own shape and colour, or just a custom name.
  • Units named after a default unit type — PFC, VAN, K9, CIU, HWY, CIRT, AV, FRV — get that type's icon and colour on the map and in the sidebar instead of the usual letter badge.
  • Tap an existing unit's pin to set En Route / Arrived and assign a task.
  • Use the draw tools (top-right) to mark and name an area or a line — like a search sector, cordon, or a path someone walked — and pick a colour for it. Finish with the toolbar's Finish button, a double-tap on the map, or by tapping the active draw tool again.
  • Tap an item in the sidebar lists to jump straight to it on the map.
  • Use the tab on the sidebar's edge, or the menu button, to free up map space.
  • On an incident, set a Subject Category to see behavioral guidance and draw statistical probability rings (25/50/75/95%) around the Last Known Position.
  • The "Layers" group in the map control panel shows/hides the % rings, Probable Route, and Theoretical Max (Naismith) ring for every incident at once — separate from each incident's own show/hide buttons in its popup.
  • On a Missing Person incident, "Theoretical Max (Naismith)" draws a pulsing dashed outer boundary of where the subject physically could not have gone beyond, based on time missing, terrain, and the subject category's typical mobility.
  • "Reflex Task Area" draws the very first-response search circle (300–500 m) around a Missing Person's location — check it before anything else, even before a subject category is set.
  • "Add Sighting" on a Missing Person incident records a confirmed sighting at a new map location — it adds a second, amber marker there and re-anchors the rings/route/Theoretical Max to it, while freezing the previous ones in place at low opacity so you can still see where the search started.
  • Mattson Consensus (top-right toolbar) scores drawn Areas against each other when several are equally plausible — have your team independently rate each one, and shade the map by the result.
  • "Search Urgency Assessment" on a Missing Person incident scores 11 factors into an Emergency/Measured/Evaluate classification — shown as a badge in the incident's map popup.
  • On a drawn Area, "Assign & Log Coverage" tracks which team searched it, at what spacing/speed/duration, and computes a real detection probability. "Split into Segments" divides a large Area for separate team assignment. Mattson Consensus combines these into a cumulative Probability of Success.

Colours

Amber is en route, blue is arrived. A deeper orange or green means that unit also has a task assigned. Red marks an incident location; purple marks a point of interest such as an exhibit.

Task codes

CI — Cordon (Inner) · CO — Cordon (Outer) · SR — Search · TM — TMP · CG — Crime Scene Guard

Data & sync

Units, incidents, points of interest, drawn shapes and lines, and presets are shared live with anyone using this map, syncing roughly every 8 seconds. Sidebar state and map style stay local to your device.

v3.97 — 19 July 2026 — the Probable Route's Overpass fetch (tracks/roads/waterways/buildings) now warns visibly if it fails, instead of silently falling back to a pure-terrain line with no awareness of streets or houses at all. This was previously invisible — a route that looked identical whether it had real track/building data or none. A common cause is opening this file from local/downloaded storage (a file:// or content:// URL) rather than a proper http/https host: the browser often can't complete a cross-origin fetch to the public Overpass API from that context, so every route silently reverted to raw terrain, ignoring every street and building on the map — exactly the "still crossing houses" symptom reported after v3.96. Hosting the file (even a simple local web server) instead of opening the downloaded copy directly should resolve it; the new toast/console warning confirms whether that's what's happening.

v3.96 — 19 July 2026 — Probable Route no longer draws through houses. The route's Overpass fetch had never queried building footprints at all, so nothing in the model knew a structure was there — with a strong pull toward the nearest track/road (which often runs right past or between houses), the line could and did get drawn straight across buildings. Building footprints (way["building"]) are now fetched alongside the existing tracks/waterways/barriers, and treated as their own hard-barrier class the route steers around or stops at, using a much tighter clearance (8 m) than rivers/cliffs (40 m) so it steps around a single house rather than being shoved wide of a whole street in built-up areas. The route's tooltip now lists buildings alongside rivers/cliffs as terrain it's steered clear of.

v3.95 — 19 July 2026 — Probable Route now models two more behaviours straight from the national manual's §3.15 "Subjective/Decision Point Strategy": (1) it's now pulled toward nearby minor waterways (streams/drains/ditches) and fence/hedge/wall lines, not just tracks/roads — §3.15.4's "funnel points" and the substantial Water/watercourse and Linear feature (fence/hedge) shares in nearly every category's own recorded find-location split had no effect on the line before this; (2) fast-flowing rivers (waterway=river) and cliffs (barrier=cliff) are now treated as hard barriers the route steers away from and stops at rather than drawing straight across — §3.15.2 lists these as terrain "which cannot be crossed or only crossed with difficulty", which previously had no representation in the model at all. The route's Overpass fetch is broadened to match, and its tooltip now names the manual section.

v3.68 — 19 July 2026 — the POS heat fill's display cutoff (v3.67) is raised from 0.12 to 0.6: most rays land close to "as expected" terrain, which after the intensity stretch sits in the green band, so green alone was covering ~95% of every ring and drowning out the actual signal underneath. Only cells at or above the yellow stop — genuinely favorable terrain/bias — are drawn now; blue and green ("as expected" or worse) are left blank. The Legend flyout's gradient bar, labels, and note were updated to match: the bar now runs yellow→orange→red only, and the labels/note reflect that this is a "favorable ground" highlight rather than a full less/more spectrum.

v3.67 — 19 July 2026 — POS heat cells scored in the lightest/coolest band (combined intensity below 0.12, after search dampening) are no longer drawn at all, instead of appearing as a faint tinted sector — that end of the scale isn't telling a searcher anything actionable, so the fill now only shows ground that's actually worth a second look. The Legend flyout's gradient bar and note were updated to match: the bar now starts at that same 0.12 cutoff color instead of showing a "nothing" blue that never appears on the map, and the note calls out that least-favorable cells are hidden rather than dimmed.

v3.66 — 19 July 2026 — three improvements to the POS heat fill. (1) Resolution: the heat grid now runs on its own 36-ray/12-radial-band sampling (10° per ray, each of the 4 statistical bands split into 3 sub-bands) instead of reusing the 24-ray/4-band grid shared with the terrain rings and Probable Route, so the gradient reads as smoothly graduated rather than 4 flat rings of solid color — the ring boundaries, rings, and route are unaffected. (2) Coverage signal: any Area or split segment marked "Searched" now dampens the heat fill inside it — toward cool and toward transparent — using that shape's own logged Coverage/POD if it has one (remaining fraction = 1 − POD), or a flat placeholder dampening if it was marked Searched without logging Coverage; a hot terrain/bias score no longer reads as "search here" once that ground has actually been swept. (3) Legend: opening the map's Legend flyout while any incident's POS heat is showing now also displays the cool→hot gradient bar with "Less favorable/More favorable" labels and a note on what dimmed cells mean; it hides again once no POS heat layer is on the map.

v3.65 — 19 July 2026 — fixed the POS heat fill (v3.64) rendering on top of the ring boundaries and hiding them: the ring and heat layers were plain L.layerGroup()s, which don't support bringToBack/bringToFront, so the call meant to keep the rings on top was silently throwing inside a promise chain — the heat layer stayed on top, and (since the throw happened before it was recorded) became untracked, so it couldn't even be cleared on the next re-render. Both layers are now L.featureGroup()s so that call actually works. Also restyled the POS ring boundaries themselves: plain black stroke with a white outline instead of the four-color POS_RING_COLORS palette, since that palette read poorly against the heat gradient underneath — the four bands are still identified via tooltip, just no longer color-coded on the ring line itself. Frozen POS-ring snapshots (on a new sighting) match the same black/white styling.

v3.64 — 19 July 2026 — POS (Probability of Success) rings no longer distort into terrain-adjusted polygons like the % rings do; their boundaries are now always plain circles at the survey-percentile distances. The terrain-ease and behavior-bias (movementBias) signal that used to bend that boundary is redirected into a heat fill inside the rings instead: the same 24-ray terrain/bias walk is run, and each of the 4×24 ring-band/direction cells is scored by how far that ray actually reached versus its band's plain statistical radius, then shaded on a cool-blue-to-hot-red gradient — easier/downhill or bias-favored directions run hot, rougher/uphill directions stay cool. Frozen POS-ring snapshots on a new sighting still keep just the plain boundaries, not a heat snapshot.

v3.63 — 19 July 2026 — corrected the POS (Probability of Success) rings added in v3.62: they now source each ring's distance the same way the % rings do — pickCategoryStats, the national manual/AU/US survey's 25/50/75/95th-percentile find-distances for the subject category — instead of the "set distance" (category walking speed × elapsed time) model shipped last version. POS rings are now a true duplicate of the % rings' geometry (same terrain refinement, timeline-scrub cap, behavior bias, and frozen-overlay treatment on a new sighting), just drawn as a separate sky/violet/fuchsia/pink dotted-stroke layer with its own toggle — laying the groundwork for blending the four discrete bands into one continuous probability-of-success heatmap, rather than differentiating POS's radii from %'s. Removed the now-unused set-distance calculation.

v3.62 — 18 July 2026 — added POS (Probability of Success) rings: a duplicate of the % probability rings (same terrain refinement, timeline scrub, and frozen-overlay treatment on a new sighting) but sourcing each ring's distance differently. The % rings use the national manual's statistical percentiles (survey data — where subjects in that category were actually found, historically). POS rings use a "set distance" instead — the subject category's own average walking speed (the same per-category mobility adjustment Theoretical Max already uses) compared against how long they've been missing, split into the same 25/50/75/95% bands, now read as fractions of that one computed distance rather than survey percentiles. New "POS" button in the control panel's Layers group (sky/violet/fuchsia/pink, dotted stroke — visually distinct from the green/blue/amber/red % rings), and a matching Show/Hide POS Rings action in the incident popup. This is a first step toward a continuous probability-of-success heat map rather than four discrete bands — the plan for a later pass.

Added three new buttons to the map control panel, under a new "Layers" group, to show/hide the % probability rings, the Probable Route line, and the Theoretical Max (Naismith) ring map-wide, independent of each incident's own per-incident toggle.

v3.7.0 — 16 July 2026 — an incident is now always required, so the sidebar's Add Incident address search is gone; the sidebar incident list no longer shows a Search Urgency Assessment badge (still available via the popup and the SUA form itself). Weather and time are now permanently one merged card, side by side at every screen size (previously stacked on mobile, and only visually merged on PC); the weather line now shows just the next upcoming sunrise/sunset instead of both. The time side of that card is wider, drops its "since LKP"/"since incident" label, and its local-clock and time-missing readouts are now matched in size/weight and colour-coded (clock black, time missing dark red) instead of one being a small muted caption.

v3.6.0 — 16 July 2026 — ported over from a parallel branch: callsign and Point of Interest Type chips can now be dragged straight onto the map to place a team or POI at that exact spot, in addition to the existing click-to-add-at-map-center behaviour; sidebar Units/POIs/Shapes sections now auto-expand the moment their first record is added instead of staying collapsed; mobile sidebar header is sized to the title only (subtitle hides, button row no longer wraps, hamburger button now sits rightmost); Reset Everything no longer leaves Reflex/Naismith rings or Trail overlays stuck on the map, and now reopens the startup wizard afterward; the startup wizard's input listeners are now guarded against being wired twice if it's reopened.

v3.5.0 — 16 July 2026 — on PC-sized screens, the weather and time widgets are now one merged card (was two stacked pills), and all their emoji icons (weather condition, sunrise/sunset, clock, elapsed-time stopwatch) are now stroke-style SVGs matching the map control panel's icon look; the NAV/DRAW/etc. section labels in that panel now sit on their own white pill background. The "Theoretical Max (Naismith)" ring now pulses like the incident marker, and its distance bound now factors in the subject category's typical mobility on top of the terrain speed (previously terrain-only) — see the mobilitySpeedFactor comment for how categories are scored.

v3.4.0 — 16 July 2026 — fixed the Probable Route silently failing to appear: the track/road lookup added in v3.2.0 had no timeout, so on a network that can't reach the public Overpass API it could hang indefinitely instead of falling back to the terrain-only model (now capped at 6s per endpoint). Also extended the same behavior-adjusted rings/route model to Child (4–6 & 7–12), Gatherer, Horseback Rider, Hunter, Mental Illness, Snowmobiler, and Youth (13–15) — each has a direct track/trail/linear-feature-following statement in its own behavior notes; see the movementBias comment on each category for the specific reference and confidence.

v3.3.0 — 16 July 2026 — on PC-sized screens (769px+ wide), the sidebar is now a permanent 420px-wide dock (50% wider than before) instead of a collapsible overlay drawer — it can no longer be slid away, so its collapse button and the map control panel's hamburger button are hidden there. The map control panel is now a single horizontal bar docked bottom-centre of the map on PC, instead of a vertical column top-left; the legend flyout now opens above it to match. Mobile/tablet layouts (below 769px) are unchanged.

v3.2.0 — 16 July 2026 — Probable Route now pulls toward nearby tracks/roads instead of running on pure terrain: it fetches OSM paths, tracks, footways, bridleways and minor/major roads around the incident and, at each step, blends the terrain-easiest heading with the bearing to the nearest such feature (the closer the feature, the stronger the pull). Reaching a track/road also counts as "not stuck" even on flat/uphill ground, so the line can keep following it rather than terminating early — consistent with the national manual's high Road/track share of find locations. Falls back to the existing pure-terrain behavior if the Overpass fetch fails.

v3.1.1 — 16 July 2026 — fixed behavior-adjusted terrain rings rendering as self-crossing "spikes" for categories with a strong downhill/path-of-least-resistance bias: cumulative heading drift is now capped so a ray curves toward easier terrain instead of spiraling, and ring vertices are re-sorted by true bearing before drawing so the polygon can't cross itself. Applies to the Probable Route line too.

v3.1.0 — 16 July 2026 — terrain-adjusted probability rings now account for subject behavior, not just walking effort: categories with a documented downhill/path-of-least-resistance tendency (Dementia/Alzheimer's, Intellectual/Developmental Disability, Hiker) drift the rings toward easier terrain instead of a flat compass bearing. New "Probable Route" line (in the incident popup, for those same categories) traces a single suggested path down the terrain, stopping where the subject would plausibly get stuck.
